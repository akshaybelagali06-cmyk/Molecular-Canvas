# 🔭 Vision — Molecular Canvas

## Mission Statement

> **To democratize molecular science by making chemistry interactive, visual, intelligent, and universally accessible.**

Molecular Canvas exists at the intersection of scientific computing, immersive visualization, and artificial intelligence. Our mission is to close the gap between how molecules truly behave and how they are traditionally taught — through static textbook diagrams.

---

## The Problem We Solve

Chemistry education and molecular research suffer from a critical visualization problem:

- **Students** struggle to understand 3D molecular geometry from 2D diagrams
- **Researchers** use expensive, proprietary software with steep learning curves
- **Educators** lack interactive tools to demonstrate reaction mechanisms in real time
- **The general public** has no accessible entry point into molecular science

Molecular Canvas solves all of this in a single, open, web-native platform.

---

## The World We Want to Build

### 🎓 For Students
A platform where chemistry becomes intuitive — where you can grab an atom, form a bond, and watch electron density shift in real time. Where an AI tutor explains why a reaction happens, not just that it happens.

### 🔬 For Researchers
A collaborative molecular workspace supporting PDB file imports, molecular dynamics simulations, force field calculations, and shareable sessions — all in the browser.

### 👩‍🏫 For Educators
Embeddable, interactive molecular scenes for lectures and assignments. Custom-built lesson flows with AI checkpoints. Export simulations as videos or interactive embeds.

### 🌍 For Everyone
A free, open-source platform that runs on any device with a browser. No software installation. No licenses. No barriers.

---

## Long-Term Vision (5+ Years)

### Phase 1 — Interactive Foundation
Build a best-in-class molecule builder and visualizer with educational AI support.

### Phase 2 — Simulation Platform
Full molecular dynamics with quantum-mechanical approximations, reaction pathway visualization, thermodynamics, and kinetics.

### Phase 3 — Collaborative Science
Real-time multiplayer molecular workspaces. Research teams collaborate live, share sessions, annotate molecules.

### Phase 4 — VR/AR Integration
Spatial computing support. Reach into a holographic molecule. Walk through a protein structure. Perform chemistry with your hands in AR.

### Phase 5 — Research Platform
Integration with PubChem, PDB, ChEMBL. AI-powered literature-to-molecule workflows. Computational experiment design.

## Core Design Principles

| Principle | Description |
|---|---|
| **Accuracy First** | All chemistry must be scientifically correct or clearly marked as approximated |
| **Accessibility** | Works on low-end devices; usable with keyboard/screen readers |
| **Openness** | MIT licensed, extensible, community-driven |
| **Beauty** | Scientific accuracy and stunning aesthetics are not mutually exclusive |
| **Performance** | Sub-16ms render loops; real-time simulation at 60fps where possible |
| **Modularity** | Every engine (molecular, rendering, physics, AI) is independently usable |
